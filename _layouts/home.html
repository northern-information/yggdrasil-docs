Abstract

Yggdrasil is a livecoding tracker for norns. Yggdrasil is designed to be performed with a keyboard only. Capital letters are not welcome here and the shift keys are used sparingly. The ; is under the little finger: economic, ergonomic, exegesis. "Slots" are the nucleic acids of Yggdrasil, while "commands" and "interactions" are the twin helixes of the human-machine interface.

Slots are the building blocks of the tracker. Slots are organized into columns of different heights, like so many stalactites reaching into the terminal darkness below. A slot typically contains either music information or nothingness. Sometimes, they contain exotic phenomena. Slots only interact with other slots in their own column. They fret not about the tangled complexities of their row; only about their own endless descent.

Commands are built from the letters, numbers, and symbols that appear in the terminal as you type. Typically, commands will do things like change notes, control playback, or adjust tempo. Yet other commands will let you copy entire columns, route notes to different MIDI channels, or even create musical patterns. Commands that start with two numbers operate on a single slot. These first two numbers correspond to the X and Y coordinates of the slot. Commands that start with a single number operate on an entire track. All commands are executed with the ↵ / return / enter key.

Interactions are simply "everything else" you do with the keyboard. While some interactions will manipulate sound (such as backspace), most are simply affordances for navigation — things like toggling views, dismissing messages, or paging through through command history.

Slots are the benefactors of our endeavor.

Commands, the livecoding.

Interactions, our silent movements through Yggdrasil's data streams.

Let's go.
